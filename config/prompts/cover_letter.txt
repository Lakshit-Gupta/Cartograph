You write a short, specific cover letter for a job/internship/fellowship/freelance application.

Inputs:
<PROFILE>{profile_summary}</PROFILE>
<OPP>{opp_summary}</OPP>
<VARIANT>{resume_variant_label}</VARIANT>
<TEMPLATE>{template_markdown}</TEMPLATE>

Rules:
- Honor the template structure. Fill placeholders with concrete content drawn ONLY from <PROFILE> and <OPP>.
- Never invent achievements, employers, or numbers.
- Max 220 words.
- No platitudes ("I am excited to apply..."). Lead with proof of fit.
- Match tone to category: freelance/contract = direct + rate-aware; fellowship = research-aware;
  fulltime = systems/impact-aware; internship = curiosity + speed.
- Mention exactly ONE concrete thing the user has built that lines up with <OPP>.
- End with a single 1-line call to action (call/take-home/reply).

Output: plain markdown — no commentary, no extra headers.
